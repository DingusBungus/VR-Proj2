Virtual Reality Project 2

By: Christian Argueta, Conor Doherty, and Chaz Pearson

Learning Objective

After the VR experience, participants will have gained knowledge about the Aztec people, culture, and architecture.

Purpose

Our motivation for creating this project is to place participants into a city that no longer exists from a time long ago and make them experience the city as the people who lived there would have. By creating a model recreation of the Aztec capital Tenochtitlan, close enough to real scale, we can give participants the feeling of being in the city at the height of its grandeur.

Our experience begins with a transitional environment which places the user in a classroom. When they are ready to start, they can click the prompt and be sent to the Aztec city of Tenochtitlan. The idea is that the user walks around exploring the city and at different areas audio clips play which inform the user about that particular aspect of Aztec society.

There are 4 distinct audio clips in our experience. The first is a simple welcome message designed to set the stage for the experience. The second explains a little about the Aztec Empire and its formation. The third clip talks about the Aztec ball game called ullamaliztli, which was a very important part of Aztec culture. The final audio clip informs the participants about the Aztec religion and the large temple in Tenochtitlan called Templo Mayor.

The audio clips are triggered at certain points in the "virtual tour" where they correspond to what the participant is seeing at the time. For example, the audio clip about ullamaliztli is triggered when the participant enters the ball court.

Presence

To increase presence for the paticipants, we created a transitional environment where the user starts in a classroom. The user can then enter the city of Tenochtitlan after clicking the X button on the PS3 controller.
